# Basecamp Coffee — Loyalty Program Turnaround

## Project Overview
I'm the new manager of Basecamp Coffee's loyalty program. I inherited a struggling
program and have **3 months to turn it around.** This file is the standing context for
that mission — read it first every session.

## The Problem
The loyalty program is a **leaky bucket.** Acquisition is healthy, but engagement is
collapsing:
- Signups grew 1,850 → 4,120, but monthly actives fell 1,200 → 480.
- Visit frequency dropped 3.8 → 1.6 visits/month.
- 30-day retention is 27%; 90-day retention just 14%.

The crucial insight: **members aren't leaving angry — they're leaving indifferent.**
Brand NPS holds steady (67 → 66) while *program* NPS craters (34 → 12). They still
love the coffee. The program became background noise.

Root cause: the program **has no personality.** It's a points tracker with no
emotional connection, no identity, no reason to care. Competitors who win
(Dutch Bros, Starbucks) lean on the human/identity layer — recognition and
personalization, not points. We capture **zero preference data** today.

## The Solution — Coffee Personality Quiz
A **"What's your coffee personality?"** experience that:
- Assigns members an identity (e.g. "Espresso Explorer").
- Drives personalized drink recommendations (fixes the discovery problem).
- Gives the app a reason to reopen.
- Is inherently shareable.
- Replaces *points-as-motivation* with **belonging.**

This idea emerged from three converging signals: the previous manager's "coffee
personalities" note, the analysis showing "no personality," and baristas hearing
"what should I try?" every shift. Identity must anchor to a **real recommendation**
a barista can deliver — not just a cute badge.

## Key Metrics to Track
- Monthly active members (currently ~480)
- Visit frequency (currently 1.6/month)
- 30-day retention (27%) and 90-day retention (14%)
- Program NPS (currently 12)
- Preference data captured (currently zero)

## Stakeholders
- **Dana** — predecessor / handoff context
- **Leadership / Exec** — cares about ROI and measurable impact. Wants a costed
  pilot with a 90-day milestone and a kill criterion, not vague "make it better."
- **Store managers / Baristas** — ground-level operations. Anything we build must be
  explainable in one sentence at the register and tied to a real recommendation.
- **Members / Customers** — indifferent, not hostile. They want to feel known and
  to belong; they keep asking "what should I try?"

## Brand Voice
Warm, human, and personality-forward — the opposite of a sterile points tracker.
Basecamp talks like a knowledgeable friend behind the counter, not a corporate
rewards email. Avoid gimmicky/cheesy — regulars can smell it.
