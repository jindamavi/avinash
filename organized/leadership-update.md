# Basecamp Rewards — Loyalty Program Turnaround
**Date:** June 17, 2026
**From:** [Your Name]

### Status
At Risk

### Summary
Basecamp Rewards is quietly failing — satisfaction is eroding month over month and the "neutral/apathetic" middle is growing. Customers love the Basecamp brand (overall NPS 67) but feel nothing about the rewards program (NPS 12). The fix is clear: the program needs personality, not more points.

### Key Metrics
| Metric | Current | Target | Trend |
|--------|---------|--------|-------|
| Rewards NPS | 12 | 50+ | Down |
| "Very Satisfied" customers | 7% (Apr) | 25% | Down |
| Neutral / apathetic | 48% (Apr) | <25% | Up (bad) |
| Brand NPS (for contrast) | 67 | Maintain | Flat |

### Progress Since Last Update
- Synthesized 4 months of customer feedback (Jan–Apr) into a clear root-cause picture
- Identified the core problem: a generic transactional points system bolted onto a brand people love *because it feels personal*
- Surfaced a customer-requested opportunity: personalization around coffee tastes ("help me find my signature drink")

### Blockers / Risks
- **Apathy is compounding** — every month more customers drift into "neutral." The longer we wait, the more expensive re-engagement gets.
- Confusing tier system ("Explorer," "Trailblazer") that not even baristas can explain.
- We're losing on *experience*, not coffee — competitors (Starbucks gamification, Dutch Bros relationships) are winning the engagement layer.

### Next Steps
- Prototype a "coffee personality" concept that turns rewards into something personal and shareable
- Test messaging that sounds like Basecamp, not "corporate stuff"
- Bring a recommended direction to the next leadership review

### Decision Needed
Approval to invest in reimagining the rewards experience around personalization (a "coffee personality" approach) rather than continuing to patch the existing points system. Recommendation: greenlight a small pilot.
