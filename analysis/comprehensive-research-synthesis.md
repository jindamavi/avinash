# Comprehensive Research Synthesis

*Four independent analyses — member data, competitors vs. our campaigns, feedback vs. competitor features, and gaps — connected into one picture.*

---

## What Each Source Revealed

### 1. The Member Data (the numbers)
It's a **leaky bucket.** Acquisition is healthy (1,850 → 4,120 signups) but monthly actives fell the opposite way (1,200 → 480). People sign up, visit a few times — frequency dropped from 3.8 to 1.6 visits/month — then fade. 30-day retention is 27%, 90-day just 14%.

The crucial detail: **they're not leaving angry — they're leaving indifferent.** Brand NPS holds steady (67 → 66) while *program* NPS craters (34 → 12). They still love the coffee. The loyalty program just became background noise they stopped noticing.

### 2. Competitors vs. Our Failed Campaigns
Everywhere we leaned on **transactions**, we failed. Everywhere competitors win, they lean on the **human/identity layer**:
- Dutch Bros & Roast & Co. — baristas remember you; you feel *seen*.
- Identity labels people broadcast ("I'm an Ethiopian natural person," "green coffee people").
- Starbucks' ~60% retention comes from **personalization**, not points. Points-only Peet's admits its program is "just there."

We stumbled on exactly the human layer they own.

### 3. Customer Feedback vs. Competitor Features
Customers are **asking for things competitors already have** — and groping toward something nobody fully owns:
- "Generic / no personality" — every single month.
- "Treated like a transaction, not valued."
- "Nothing fun" — they literally cite *"Starbucks at least has games."*
- And the vague one: *"help me find new drinks," "what kind of coffee person am I?"* — they want **discovery-as-identity** but can't name it.

### 4. The Gaps
The biggest unaddressed opportunity is the one nobody in our market is doing: **identity-based loyalty.** Every signal converges on it — the survey data (08), the social-share instinct (09), the streak-author's own pivot (10), even the previous manager's nagging quiz idea.

---

## Connecting the Dots — The Discoveries

Looking at these *together* reveals what no single source showed:

1. **People sign up, visit 2–3 times, then disappear — not angry, just indifferent.** (data + feedback)
2. **Competitors with personality/identity features retain ~3x better than points-only programs.** (competitors + data)
3. **Customers keep asking for "something more" but can't articulate what.** (feedback)
4. **The gap: nobody in our market has a personality-based approach.** (gaps)

Each agent saw a piece. Stacked together, they all point at the same missing thing.

---

## The Whitespace

- Customers are **indifferent**, not hostile — which means the door is still open.
- They want to feel **known and to belong**, not to earn points.
- Competitors deliver *recognition* and *identity* piecemeal — but **no one owns "discovery-as-identity."**
- We capture **zero preference data** today, so we can't personalize even if we wanted to.

The convergent answer: a **"What's your coffee personality?"** experience — assign an identity ("Espresso Explorer"), drive personalized recommendations (fixes discovery), give the app a reason to reopen, and make it inherently shareable. Replace *points-as-motivation* with *belonging.*

That's the through-line every source has been pointing at.
