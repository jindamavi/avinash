# Campaign Salvage Report

*Analysis of 10 inherited loyalty campaigns — what to keep, what to kill, and the pattern underneath them all.*

---

## The Pattern Across All 10

Every single campaign was about driving **MORE TRANSACTIONS** — double points, bonus points, multipliers, streaks. Not one was about making people *feel* something. They were all transactional. Nothing touched identity, belonging, or a reason to care.

The previous manager kept reaching for the same lever (points) and pointing it at the same problem (engagement), and it kept not working — because points aren't the thing people are missing.

---

## Campaign-by-Campaign

### 01 — Double Points Weekend
- **Mechanic:** 2x points on a Fri–Sun window.
- **Outcome:** Ran twice. +23% app scans during promo, back to baseline within 3 days. ~$2,400 in added liability, no retention lift.
- **Verdict:** ❌ Weak alone — classic "discount treadmill." Bought temporary behavior, built no loyalty.
- **Salvage:** Only if made exclusive (members-only) or paired with a habit-forming element.

### 02 — Happy Hour Points Boost
- **Mechanic:** 3x points on slow afternoons, pushed via 1:30pm notifications.
- **Outcome:** 8% afternoon lift; 34% of pushes dismissed instantly; complaints ("Stop texting me to buy coffee").
- **Verdict:** ⚠️ Incentive fine, delivery felt spammy and eroded goodwill.
- **Salvage:** Reframe as opt-in/ambient/geofenced rather than push-blasting.

### 03 — Enhanced Birthday Rewards
- **Mechanic:** Free drink + 50 points + personalized email in birthday month.
- **Outcome:** 62% redemption, 31% open rate — but 73% of redeemers were already regulars. Didn't reactivate lapsed members.
- **Verdict:** ⚠️ Table stakes; every competitor does it. Rewarded engagement that already existed.
- **Salvage:** The *insight* is gold — personal/emotional touches beat transactional points.

### 04 — Refer a Friend
- **Mechanic:** Referral codes, bonus points for both parties.
- **Outcome:** Never launched. Died in legal review + unsolved tracking + dev deprioritization.
- **Verdict:** ⚠️ Killed by friction; also strategically weak as pure reward-for-signup.
- **Salvage:** Yes, *if* the hook is identity-driven and shareable (e.g., a quiz people want to pass along) rather than points-driven.

### 05 — Early Bird Bonus
- **Mechanic:** 1.5x points before 8am, daily.
- **Outcome:** Never ran. Killed in draft.
- **Verdict:** ❌ Backwards economics — subsidizes behavior already happening (morning regulars).
- **Salvage:** Point the lever at *new* behavior (off-peak hours, or win-back for disengaged members).

### 06 — Seasonal Drink Launch Points
- **Mechanic:** 2x points on seasonal drinks, launch week.
- **Outcome:** Trial up to 31% (from 22%), but only 12% repurchased. ~$800.
- **Verdict:** ⚠️ Novelty drove trial, not the points. No mechanism to convert triers to repeat buyers.
- **Salvage:** Reframe around personalized recommendations ("if you like X, you'll love Y") — requires preference data.

### 07 — App Download Bonus
- **Mechanic:** 25 points for download + registration.
- **Outcome:** ~400 downloads/month, but only 31% make a second transaction. 69% grab the bonus and vanish.
- **Verdict:** ❌ Rewarded the wrong behavior (installs, not engagement). App has no reason to reopen.
- **Salvage:** Reward engagement over installation, and give the app a reason to exist — personalization, content, a "coffee personality" feature.

### 08 — Survey Completion Bonus
- **Mechanic:** 15 points for a 3-minute satisfaction survey.
- **Outcome:** 23% completion. Cheap. Recurring verdict from members: the program is "fine"/forgettable.
- **Verdict:** ⚠️ Measures sentiment but doesn't create it. Diagnosis without treatment.
- **Salvage:** **High** — not the survey, the *data*. Members surfaced two buried gems: they want a "coffee personality" discovery experience and a Basecamp-like *feel*, not generic rewards.

### 09 — Social Share Bonus
- **Mechanic:** Share content → screenshot → show barista → 20 points.
- **Outcome:** Disaster. 12 total redemptions, zero organic engagement, embarrassed customers, annoyed staff.
- **Verdict:** ❌ Forced, transactional, clunky. People felt *used* for marketing.
- **Salvage:** Don't pay for shares — create something genuinely shareable. Identity/status moments ("I'm an Espresso Explorer!") people *want* to broadcast.

### 10 — Visit Streak Bonus
- **Mechanic:** Escalating points for consecutive-day visits + "Streak Champion" badge.
- **Outcome:** Never launched. Killed by self-awareness (gaming risk, daily coffee isn't Duolingo).
- **Verdict:** ❌ Manufactured artificial engagement.
- **Salvage:** The author's own pivot says it: replace frequency with **variety and discovery**, shift from transactions to emotional ownership — make the shop feel like *theirs*.

---

## Worth Salvaging — Reframed

The mechanics mostly belong in the trash. The recurring *ideas* underneath them are the treasure:

1. **Identity & status** — people want to be an "Espresso Explorer," not collect points (from 03, 08, 09, 10).
2. **Discovery over repetition** — help people find new things they'll love, not just visit more (06, 10).
3. **Personal recognition** — emotional touches beat transactional rewards (03).
4. **A reason to open the app** — personalization/content, not a points balance (07).
5. **Shareable by design** — make something people *want* to pass along (04, 09).

## The Through-Line

Notice what keeps surfacing — buried in the survey data (08), in the streak author's pivot (10), in the social-share instinct (09): **coffee personalities.** Customers don't want more points. They want to know *who they are* as a coffee drinker, and they want this place to feel like theirs.
